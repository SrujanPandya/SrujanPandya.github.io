All-Time Favorites

This is a personal list of the All-Time Favorite movies.
These are either masterpieces, best screenplays, visual spectacles, an unforgettable vibe or simply a great watch.

A Note to Fellow Cinephiles

Hi, I'm a human! So before you judge me for my personal list of all-time favorites, I want to make it clear that you are entitled to your views, opinions, arguments, choices and comments, just the way I am. And hence, take these as my all-time favorites and not the all-time favorties you have in your mind.
P.S. You can always message me if I miss out on some good ones.

12 Angry Men

I have to start with this one. Probably the best directorial debut in the history of cinema. The beauty of this film lies in its simplicity - both in its plot and the execution. You don't need to have a major in physics to understand it, unlike many of Nolan's films (just kidding! I love Nolan.). The crisp runtime and the tight one-room setting (ignoring for the first few minutes) makes it a gripping moral tale of twist and turns.

The Prestige

I'd go bold and say that this is Nolan's best work in my opinion. While I do recognise the greatness of Interstellar, Inception and The Dark Knight, this film has a special place in my heart and mind. Everything about this film is ten on ten: acting, setting, editing, direction and the exceptional storytelling that Nolan does before he turned to physics for his movies. Highly underrated as well as highly underappreciated. Not a hidden gem, but surely an overlooked one.

Memories of Murder

Non-English Entry! Bong Joon-Ho's 2003 classic. Fun Fact: Oldboy - another classic - was also released in the same year and it'd be very tough for me if someone asks me to choose from these two. Similar to Zodiac in some sense. True Story. Serial Killers. Unsolved mytery solved years later. However, thematically I find Memories of Murder more nuanced, with one of the best endings I've ever seen.
