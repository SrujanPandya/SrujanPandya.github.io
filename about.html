About

This is a space for thinking in public—essays that attempt to make sense of contemporary life, film reviews that treat cinema as a serious art form, and blog posts that capture passing thoughts and observations before they dissolve.

I write about philosophy, culture, art, and the everyday experiences that reveal something larger about how we live now. My interests tend toward the contemplative, the slow, and the overlooked. I'm drawn to questions that don't have easy answers and ideas that resist simple summary.

Background

I hold a degree in comparative literature and have published essays in various literary journals and magazines. My work has appeared in The Hedgehog Review, Los Angeles Review of Books, and Film Comment, among others.

When I'm not writing, I teach courses on film theory and modern literature at a small liberal arts college. I also maintain a longstanding practice of keeping notebooks—commonplace books filled with quotations, observations, and half-formed thoughts that sometimes find their way into more polished pieces.

About This Site

I created this portfolio as a home for my writing across different forms and subjects. The Essays section contains longer, more researched pieces on philosophy, culture, and contemporary life. Movies features film reviews and essays on cinema. The Blog is for shorter reflections, reading notes, and personal observations.

The design of this site is intentionally minimal, inspired by publications like Aeon and The Paris Review. I wanted something that gets out of the way and lets the writing breathe—plenty of whitespace, clear typography, and a focus on readability above all else.

Contact

I'm always interested in hearing from readers. You can reach me at hello@example.com or find me on Twitter, where I occasionally share links and thoughts that don't warrant longer treatment.

If you'd like to stay updated on new writing, you can subscribe to the RSS feed or newsletter, which goes out monthly with new essays and recommendations.
